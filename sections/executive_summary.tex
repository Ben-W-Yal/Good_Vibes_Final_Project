\section*{Executive Summary}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Executive Summary}

\textbf{Repository URL:} \url{https://github.com/Ben-W-Yal/Good_Vibes_Final_Project}

\textbf{Main takeaway:} Combining interactive geospatial visualization with citation-grounded generative briefs helps users move faster from raw headlines, wire copy, and \textbf{social-media-surfaced events} to defensible written assessments tied to place and URLs.

\textbf{Who this is for:} Students, conflict and OSINT observers, journalists, and technically minded analysts who need one workspace to relate fast-moving signals---including those that first break on social platforms---to geography and evidence.

GeoIntel is a geospatial intelligence workstation built with TypeScript/React, Express, and Gemini-powered generative AI. It fuses open-source and news-indexed signals (many of which originate as or are amplified by \textbf{social posts}) with tracker overlays and structured AI outputs so users interpret what is happening, why it matters, and what to watch next. Instead of passively scanning disconnected feeds, users work on an interactive 3D globe with optional terrain and buildings (Cesium ion), event filters, and briefing panels.

The interface pairs narrative intelligence with geographic grounding: grounded web research surfaces article imagery where available; users pan, tilt, and zoom through the environment where events unfold. Selecting an event opens a streamed \textbf{AI analyst summary} and optional \textbf{chat} scoped to that record. That combination supports a clearer mental map of borders, urban fabric, and coastlines---relating headlines and viral narratives to real geography, not abstract place names alone.

\textbf{Design rhythm:} the UI keeps stable geographic and feed context while users add refreshed markers, regenerated PDB sections, and per-event summaries. That mirrors manual analysis: maintain a working picture, update when credible signals arrive, without claiming a formal latent-state model in code.
